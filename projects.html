Each of these projects was driven by a simple belief: nonprofits deserve the same quality of CRM strategy and implementation that any enterprise client would receive. Here's what that looked like in practice.

Project 01
New Moms

Empowering New Moms

Salesforce Consultant

For years, New Moms, a Chicago-based nonprofit supporting young mothers and their children, relied on a legacy CRM that fractured critical program and donor data across systems. Staff spent hours reconciling spreadsheets, emails, and paper records just to understand participant progress or prepare reports. Frontline teams couldn't get a full picture of a family's journey, while administrators struggled to prioritize tasks or discern which services were having the greatest impact — inefficiencies that risked slowing services for families when timing mattered most. I led the design and implementation of a unified Salesforce platform to consolidate program, case management, and donor data into a single source of truth.

Impact

Administrative workload dropped an estimated 30% as tasks that once required multiple systems now happened automatically within Salesforce. Case managers gained instant access to participant histories, cutting meeting prep from hours to minutes. Unified dashboards accelerated decision-making across program performance, donor engagement, and service delivery. Staff morale rose noticeably — freed from tedious data wrangling, team members reported renewed energy for direct client work. Team leads quickly began using real-time data to identify emerging needs, share outcomes with funders, and demonstrate measurable impact.

Project 02
Chicago Scholarships

Enforcing Approvals at Chicago Scholarships

Salesforce Consultant

Chicago Scholarships had a clearly documented approval process on paper, but in practice, staff routinely worked around it, jumping straight to communicating decisions, preparing payments, or updating records based on email threads and ad hoc conversations instead of moving through defined review and approval steps. The result was a growing disconnect between the official workflow and the way work actually got done. I led the design of a unified Salesforce platform to close that gap.

Impact

The engagement rebuilt the scholarship approval process as a gated, system-enforced workflow rather than a set of guidelines. We designed a single end-to-end workflow from application intake through award, disbursement, and close-out, with explicit approval checkpoints at each critical step. Each gate (eligibility, program review, committee recommendation, compliance, funding, and final award) was tied to named approvers, clear decision criteria, and required documentation, ensuring work could no longer bypass the process.

Project 03
Keck ACCN

From Manual Chaos to Meaningful Engagement: How a Nonprofit for Medical Professionals Transformed Operations with Salesforce Automation

Salesforce Consultant

For years, the nonprofit sat at a familiar crossroads: scaling membership growth while wrestling with disjointed systems and outdated manual processes. Its community, medical professionals balancing long shifts, continuing education, and patient care, depended on timely membership renewals and event access. Yet those same members had little time for administrative forms or follow-up emails. Behind the scenes, staff were spending valuable hours re-entering Eventbrite registrations into spreadsheets, chasing down expired memberships, and sending renewal reminders one by one.

The organization's leadership recognized a fundamental misalignment between staff effort and mission impact. They weren't just losing time. They were losing the capacity to serve members strategically. A modern, integrated platform wasn't a luxury; it was essential to becoming the trusted partner their members needed.

Impact

The Eventbrite-to-Salesforce integration became the backbone of this transformation. Previously, staff manually exported attendee lists and reconciled them with membership data. After integration, every registration flowed automatically into Salesforce. Staff could now see who attended which events, view their membership status in real time, and follow up strategically instead of reactively. The result was immediate clarity, and hours of administrative work saved each week.

Next came membership renewal automation. Using Salesforce workflows and Stripe integration, the organization transformed a labor-intensive process into a seamless experience. Members received timely renewal reminders based on their expiration dates, could pay securely online, and triggered instant notifications for staff once payment cleared. What once took days of coordination collapsed into seconds, giving both members and staff a new level of certainty and control.

Project 04
Furniture Bank

How The Furniture Bank Streamlined Donor Engagement with Salesforce Experience Cloud

Salesforce Consultant

The Furniture Bank is a nonprofit that helps families furnish their homes after experiencing homelessness, domestic violence, or other hardships. When I came in, their furniture donor intake process was entirely manual. Staff would receive donation offers through an online form, then spend time reviewing each submission individually, re-entering the information into Salesforce by hand, and following up with donors to confirm pickup details. During busy periods this created a real backlog. Donors who offered furniture could wait days for a response, and with no automated tracking in place, submissions occasionally slipped through the cracks. Leadership knew the process needed to change but didn't have a clear picture of what a better system would look like inside Salesforce. That's where I came in.

Impact

I worked with the team to rebuild their donor intake experience from the ground up using Salesforce Experience Cloud. The new form was designed with donors in mind, walking them through each step clearly and collecting all the details the team needed upfront, things like furniture type, condition, quantity, and pickup availability.


On the back end, I built automation that triggered as soon as a form was submitted. A new opportunity record was created in Salesforce automatically, populated with the donor's information and routed to the right staff member for follow-up. The team no longer had to manually enter anything. They could log in each morning and see exactly what came in overnight, who needed to be contacted, and which pickups were ready to schedule. Response times improved, fewer donations fell through the cracks, and staff could focus their energy on coordinating logistics rather than managing data entry.
